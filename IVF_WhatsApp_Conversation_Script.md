# IVF AI Lead Generation — Master Conversation Script
### WhatsApp + ElevenLabs AI Voice
*Designed for high-conversion, emotionally intelligent fertility lead generation*

---

## IMPORTANT DESIGN PRINCIPLES

Before reading: every message below is written for WhatsApp's reading pattern — short, punchy, one idea per bubble. Users read on their phone in 3 seconds. If a message is too long, it will be skipped.

**Core rules baked into every stage:**
- One question per message. Never two.
- Every answer gets a 1-line clinical insight before the next question. This builds trust faster than any CTA.
- No dead ends. Every response — even "I don't know" — has a path forward.
- Never ask for name/phone until emotional trust is established (Stage 6+).

## ═══════════════════════════════════════
## STAGE 1: HOOK — ENTRY MESSAGE (3 A/B VERSIONS)
## ═══════════════════════════════════════

*Trigger: User sends any first message (hi / hello / I want to know more / etc.)*
*No permission asking. No "Hello! I am an AI assistant." Straight into value.*

---

### VERSION A — Emotional Tone (Warm, Personal)

> "Hi 👋 Most couples who reach out to us have been trying for a while — and the hardest part is not knowing *why*.
>
> Let me ask you 3 quick questions. Based on your answers, I can tell you exactly where you stand.
>
> Is that okay?"

*If user says yes / ok / sure → move to Stage 2*
*If user says "how does this work" or asks a question → respond:*
> "It's simple — I'll ask about your situation, and based on your answers I'll give you a clinical picture and suggest the right next step. Takes 3 minutes. Ready?"

---

### VERSION B — Clinical Tone (Direct, Expert)

> "Welcome. I'm going to run a quick fertility assessment — 3 questions, takes 2 minutes.
>
> Based on your answers, you'll know:
> ✅ Where you are in your fertility journey
> ✅ What's likely causing the delay
> ✅ What your best next step is
>
> Starting now 👇"

*No permission needed. Jump straight to Stage 2, Question 1.*

---

### VERSION C — Curiosity Hook (for cold/unaware leads)

> "Did you know that 85% of couples who've been trying for over a year have at least one identifiable and treatable factor?
>
> The problem isn't always what you think.
>
> I can help you find out in 3 questions. Want to try?"

*If user says yes → Stage 2*
*If user is skeptical ("what is this?") →*
> "This is a private fertility assessment. No sales pitch — just clinical clarity. I'll ask about your situation and tell you what it likely means. You decide what to do next."

## ═══════════════════════════════════════
## STAGE 2: QUALIFICATION WITH CLINICAL INSIGHTS
## ═══════════════════════════════════════

*Each question → user answers → bot gives 1-line clinical insight → next question*
*This rhythm builds credibility with every exchange.*

---

### Q1 — TRYING DURATION

**Bot message:**
> "First question:
> How long have you been trying to conceive?
> (Even a rough answer is fine — months or years)"

---

**RESPONSE HANDLING + CLINICAL INSIGHTS:**

| User says | Clinical insight + next step |
|---|---|
| Less than 6 months | → "Most couples conceive within 12 months naturally — you're still in the normal window. But an early check never hurts." |
| 6–12 months | → "You're approaching the point where a basic fertility panel can save you 6–12 months of uncertainty. Very common to find something fixable here." |
| 1–2 years | → "After 12 months, there's almost always a specific, identifiable reason. The good news: once found, most are treatable." |
| 2–3 years | → "At this stage, the body has usually shown us what it needs. A targeted evaluation gives you a clear path." |
| 3+ years | → "Long duration often points to either an undetected structural factor or a response pattern — both of which respond well to the right treatment." |
| "I don't know" / vague | → "No worries — even an approximate sense helps. Has it been less than a year, or longer?" |
| Off-topic | → "I understand — this can feel like a lot. Let me just ask: roughly how long have you been trying? Even 'about a year' helps." |

**After insight, continue:**
> "Got it. Second question →"

---

### Q2 — AGE

**Bot message:**
> "How old are you? (Or your partner — whichever is most relevant)"

---

**RESPONSE HANDLING + CLINICAL INSIGHTS:**

| User says | Clinical insight + next step |
|---|---|
| Under 28 | → "At your age, fertility is generally strong. If there's a delay, we usually find the cause quickly — and it's almost always correctable." |
| 28–32 | → "You're in a good fertility window. An early evaluation now keeps all your options open." |
| 33–35 | → "This is a key age to get clarity. Ovarian reserve starts shifting after 35 — knowing your numbers now helps you plan with confidence." |
| 36–38 | → "After 35, egg quality becomes more important than quantity. An AMH + AFC test gives you your exact picture in 24 hours." |
| 39–41 | → "Every 6 months matters at this stage. The right protocol at this age can still achieve strong outcomes — but timing the next step matters." |
| 42+ | → "Age affects egg quality more than count. Many women in your age range succeed — but the approach needs to be precise and personalised." |
| Won't say / "Why do you need that?" | → "Completely fair — it's optional. Age helps me give you a more accurate clinical picture, but we can continue without it." → proceed to Q3 |
| Gives partner's age | → Use the same table above. Note internally: partner age given. |

---

### Q3 — TREATMENT HISTORY

**Bot message:**
> "Have you done any fertility treatment before?
> (IUI, IVF, medications — anything counts)"

---

**RESPONSE HANDLING + CLINICAL INSIGHTS:**

| User says | Clinical insight + next step |
|---|---|
| "No" / "Nothing" | → "Most couples haven't. A basic fertility panel — just 3–4 tests — usually gives complete clarity on both partners." |
| "We did IUI" | → "IUI is a great first step. If it hasn't worked in 2–3 cycles, the next step is usually a more targeted evaluation to understand why." |
| "IUI didn't work" | → "That's actually useful information. Unsuccessful IUI often points to a specific factor — sperm quality, tube function, or timing — that IVF can bypass directly." |
| "We did IVF" | → "Was the IVF cycle successful, or are you following up?" → branch below |
| "IVF failed" / "IVF didn't work" | → "A failed IVF cycle always tells us something important — about embryo quality, uterine receptivity, or protocol fit. That knowledge is the foundation for success." |
| "Multiple IVF failures" | → "After multiple cycles, the answer almost always lies in changing the approach — protocol, clinic, or diagnostic depth — not in trying harder." |
| Confused / "What do you mean?" | → "Just checking — have you ever taken fertility injections, done an intrauterine procedure, or attempted IVF? Even medication counts." |

### Q4 — CYCLE REGULARITY (Female Factor Signal)

> "One more quick one — are her periods regular?
> (Regular = roughly every 28–35 days)"

| Answer | Insight |
|---|---|
| Yes, regular | → "Regular cycles are a positive sign — they suggest ovulation is likely occurring." |
| Irregular / missed | → "Irregular cycles are one of the most common and treatable causes of fertility delays — often linked to PCOS or hormonal imbalance." |
| "I'm not sure" | → "Worth tracking. An irregular pattern, even mild, can be a key signal." |
| "She has PCOS" | → "PCOS is the most common fertility-related condition — and one of the most successfully treated. You're not alone." |

---

### Q5 — MALE FACTOR AWARENESS

> "Has a semen analysis been done for the male partner?"

| Answer | Insight |
|---|---|
| Yes, normal | → "Good to know. In that case, we'll focus the evaluation on the female side." |
| Yes, abnormal | → "Male factor is involved in 40–50% of fertility cases. The good news: most abnormalities are correctable or workable-around." |
| No | → "This is the most important test to do first — it's non-invasive, takes 1 day, and rules out 40% of all fertility causes immediately." |
| Uncomfortable / avoided the question | → "Completely understandable. It's more common than people realise, and entirely private. We can discuss this with the doctor if preferred." |

---

> "Do you have any fertility reports or test results already?

| Answer | Insight |
|---|---|

| "Some, not sure what they mean" | → "That's actually the most common situation. Send them over and the doctor will explain what they mean for your specific case." |

## STAGE 4: CLINICAL POSITIONING
## ═══════════════════════════════════════

*Used when user is confused about their situation or asks "what is wrong with us?" — delivered in stages, not all at once.*

---

**Egg:**
> "Egg quality and count are the most age-sensitive factors. An AMH blood test and an AFC scan (done during a routine ultrasound) give a precise picture in 24 hours. It doesn't mean anything is wrong — it means you know your starting point."

**Sperm:**
> "A semen analysis checks count, movement, and shape. All three matter. It takes one day and is the single most important test to do early — it rules out or confirms 40% of all fertility causes."

**Tubes & Uterus:**
> "The HSG test (a simple X-ray with dye) checks whether the fallopian tubes are open and the uterus is normal. Blocked tubes are one of the most common silent causes of unexplained infertility — and are often completely undetected without this test."

**The full picture:**
> "Most couples who've been trying a while haven't done all three checks together. When we do — egg, sperm, tubes — we find a specific, addressable factor in over 80% of cases."

### TYPE 1 — SOFT CTA (Early Stage)
*When user needs education, not urgency*

**Emotional version:**
> "Based on what you've shared, I think a free fertility assessment would give you and your partner real clarity — not guesswork.
>
> It's a 20-minute consultation. No commitment. Just answers.
>
> Would that be helpful?"

**Clinical version:**
> "At your stage, a baseline fertility panel is the most useful next step. It checks egg reserve, sperm parameters, and tube patency — and gives you a full picture in 48 hours.
>
> We offer this as a free initial consultation. Shall I check availability?"

---

### TYPE 2 — MEDIUM CTA (Mid Stage)
*When user has tried without success*

**Emotional version:**
> "You've been patient, and you've tried. But trying longer without knowing *why* it hasn't worked isn't the answer — clarity is.
>
> Dr. [Name] sees cases like yours every week. A 30-minute consultation with her can tell you more than another 6 months of waiting.
>
> Can I arrange a call for you this week?"

**Clinical version:**
> "Given your duration and history, a specialist consultation is the clinically appropriate next step. We can complete a targeted review of your previous tests and chart a specific treatment path.
>
> I have slots available this week. Should I reserve one?"

---

### TYPE 3 — STRONG CTA (Advanced)
*When user has failed IVF or is 38+*

**Emotional version:**
> "I want to be honest with you — every month matters at your stage, not because it's hopeless, but because the right protocol at the right time makes a real difference.
>
> Our team has helped couples in similar situations. But the first step is always a personalised review — not a standard protocol.
>
> I have 2 slots with Dr. [Name] this week. These go quickly. Can I reserve one for you now?"

**Clinical version:**
> "Failed IVF cycles are not a final verdict — they are diagnostic data. The next cycle's success depends entirely on what we learn from the last one.
>
> Dr. [Name] specialises in recurrent IVF failure. She has limited consultation slots this week.
>
> I'd like to reserve one for you before they're gone. What's your preferred time — morning or evening?"

---

### 3 CTA VARIATIONS (A/B Testing)

## STAGE 7: LEAD CAPTURE
## ═══════════════════════════════════════

*Never ask name and phone together. Never make it feel like a form. Earn each detail.*

---

**Step 1 — Name (after CTA accepted):**

> "To reserve your slot, I just need a couple of details.
>
> What's your name? (First name is fine)"

*If user gives full name → use first name only going forward*
*If user hesitates:*
> "Completely confidential — it's only used so the doctor knows who she's speaking with."

---

**Step 2 — Phone (after name captured):**

> "And the best number to reach you on, [First Name]?
>
> Our counsellor will call you to confirm the time — it's a quick 2-minute call."

*If user hesitates:*
> "We only call once to confirm. No follow-up unless you ask for it."

*If invalid number:*
> "That doesn't look quite right — could you double-check the number? We want to make sure we reach you."

*After phone confirmed:*
> "Perfect, [First Name]. You're all set. ✅
>
> Dr. [Name] will be in touch within [X hours / by tomorrow morning].
>
> Is there anything specific you'd like her to know before your consultation?"

*This final open question captures extra context and increases show-up rate by making the user feel heard.*

## SECTION 12: IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
## ═══════════════════════════════════════

**Message delay:** Add a 1–2 second artificial delay between messages. Instant replies feel robotic. 800ms–1.5s per message feels human.

**Typing indicator:** If Twilio supports it, trigger "typing..." before each response. Increases perceived humanity.

**Emoji use:** 1 emoji per 2–3 messages max. More than that reads as spam. Use 💙 🙏 ✅ only.

**Time of day:** Highest response rates for IVF leads are 8–10am and 8–10pm. Schedule follow-up messages in these windows.

**Language:** If user responds in Hindi or Hinglish, match that register. A user who writes "haan" and "nahi" should not get back perfect Oxford English.

**Do not:**
- Mention success rates ("We have an 80% success rate") — this is regulated speech
- Make clinical claims ("This definitely means X") — always hedge with "often", "in most cases", "this is a signal that"
- Pressure the user more than twice on the same CTA — if they decline twice, back off and leave the door open
